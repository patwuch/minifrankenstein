Mary Shelley challenged the gender and aesthetic norms of her time through her groundbreaking work and unconventional life. As the author of Frankenstein, Shelley defies expectations by writing in the male-dominated genres of Gothic horror and science fiction. Frankenstein contains critiques of patriarchy and subverts many gender expectations by exploring themes of creation and power in the form of a monstrous birth by a male scientist. Mary's personal life was equally defiant of societal norms, as she engaged in a controversial relationship with the still married Percy Bysshe Shelley, challenging the strict moral codes of her time. This manuscript of Frankenstein containing edits made by both Mary Shelley and Percy Shelley can be seen as a commentary on the bold rejection of the restrictive roles assigned to women
